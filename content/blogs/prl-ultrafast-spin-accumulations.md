Our paper **"Ultrafast Spin Accumulations Drive Magnetization Reversal in Multilayers"** has been published in **Physical Review Letters 136, 056701 (2026)**.

![Time-resolved magneto-optical measurements of spin accumulation in magnetic multilayers](/images/pubs/prl25.png)

## What the paper is about

Controlling heat and spin transport on the femtosecond timescale opens the door to manipulating magnetization at speeds far beyond conventional electronics. The problem is that the mechanisms behind this ultrafast reversal have stayed poorly understood, simply because probing spin dynamics that far out of equilibrium is hard.

In this work we show that **magneto-optical experiments can be used to follow the time evolution of the spin accumulation** generated inside a magnetic multilayer after an ultrafast laser pulse. That gives direct access to a quantity that had mostly been inferred indirectly.

The main result: **demagnetization- and remagnetization-driven spin accumulation is the key mechanism behind all-optical switching**. In a spin valve, the final magnetic state of the free layer turns out to be dictated mainly by the ultrafast dynamics of the *reference* layer — not by the free layer itself. That is a useful design principle for anyone engineering ultrafast spintronic devices.
