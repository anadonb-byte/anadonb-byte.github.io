The **1st International Symposium on Three-Dimensional Nanomagnetism (3DMAG 2026)** took place from **13 to 17 July 2026** at the Freihaus building of **TU Wien**, in Vienna. I took part as a member of the **local organising team**, hosted by the 3DNANO group at the Institute of Applied Physics, with Prof. **Amalio Fernández-Pacheco** as conference chair. The photo above shows the organising team at the closing session.

## Why a symposium on 3D nanomagnetism

3DMAG was created to bring together, for the first time under one roof, the growing international community working on magnetism in three dimensions. The central idea of the meeting is that at the nanoscale **geometry, topology, curvature, surfaces and volume stop being constraints and become active design parameters** — knobs you can use to engineer magnetic behaviour rather than properties you simply inherit from the material.

That framing is exactly the space my own work lives in: moving magnetic textures such as skyrmions through three-dimensional nanostructures, and using heat and spin currents as the driving force.

## The programme

The week combined an inaugural lecture by **Peter Fischer** (Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory) with keynote talks from **Stuart Parkin** (MPI of Microstructure Physics), **Shunsuke Fukami** (Tohoku University), **Denis Sheka** (Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv), **Claire Donnelly** (MPI for Chemical Physics of Solids) and **Dirk Grundler** (EPFL), alongside invited talks, oral contributions and a large poster session.

Being on the organising side of a conference is a very different experience from attending one. You see the meeting from the inside: the schedule, the rooms, the coffee, the hundred small things that have to work so that everyone else can simply focus on the science. It was a lot of work and genuinely worth it — the discussions during the week made it clear that this community had been waiting for a dedicated forum.

Many thanks to everyone who came to Vienna, and to the sponsors who made it possible to support so many students.
